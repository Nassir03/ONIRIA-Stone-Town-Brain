# Pre-Arrival, Arrival, and Check-In Workflows

## Purpose

This note defines how ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel prepares for a guest before arrival, welcomes the guest, completes check-in, provides room access, and handles delays or missing information.

The workflow connects reservations, front office, housekeeping, rooms, maintenance, transport, food and beverage, spa, security, payment, privacy, and Lulu AI.

# Workflow Overview

```text
Booking confirmed
→ Pre-arrival review
→ Guest information check
→ Room preparation
→ Department coordination
→ Guest arrival
→ Reservation and identity confirmation
→ Payment and registration
→ Room access
→ Hotel orientation
→ Check-in completion
→ Follow-up

Part 2: Guest Arrival Workflow
Objective

Welcome the guest safely, respectfully, and efficiently.

Trigger

The guest reaches:

Hotel entrance
Reception
Approved arrival point
Hotel transport drop-off point
Step 1: Acknowledge the Guest

The guest should be acknowledged quickly.

Staff should:

Greet the guest politely
Introduce themselves when appropriate
Offer assistance
Direct the guest to reception
Support luggage handling
Identify accessibility needs
Avoid discussing private information publicly
Step 2: Confirm the Guest or Booking

Ask for the minimum required information, such as:

Guest name
Booking reference
Approved identification

Do not announce sensitive information where other guests can hear.

Step 3: Check Arrival Status

Confirm:

Reservation found
Room assigned
Room ready
Payment status
Special requests
Transport completion
Number of guests
Arrival time
Step 4: Handle Early Arrival

When the guest arrives before the room is ready:

Confirm the reservation.
Explain the situation politely.
Avoid promising an unsupported completion time.
Offer an approved waiting solution.
Support luggage storage when authorised.
Notify housekeeping or the responsible team.
Track room readiness.
Update the guest.
Escalate unreasonable delay.
Possible Approved Support
Waiting area
Luggage storage
Refreshment
Hotel facilities
Alternative room
Room upgrade

Some options may require approval.

Step 5: Handle Missing Reservation

When the reservation cannot be found:

Confirm spelling of the guest name.
Ask for the booking reference.
Check booking REFERENCE.
Check arrival and departure dates.
Check alternative guest name.
Check approved booking systems.
Notify the front-office supervisor.
Avoid blaming the guest or booking provider.
Do not create a new booking without checking availability and approval.
Step 6: Handle Walk-In Guest

For a walk-in guest:

Check room availability.
Confirm room type.
Confirm rate.
Explain booking conditions.
Collect required guest information.
Confirm payment or guarantee.
Create the reservation.
Continue through the normal check-in process.

Discounts, unusual rates, or exceptions require approval.
