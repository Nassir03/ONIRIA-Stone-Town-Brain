# Guest Service Standards and Communication Templates

## Purpose

This note defines the service and communication standards expected when ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel staff interact with guests.

The standards apply to:

- Face-to-face communication
- Telephone calls
- Email
- WhatsApp
- Internal guest-service messages
- Delay notifications
- Complaint acknowledgement
- Service recovery
- Follow-up communication
- Lulu AI-generated drafts

## Core Service Principles

Every guest interaction should be:

- Respectful
- Clear
- Warm
- Professional
- Accurate
- Calm
- Private
- Timely
- Consistent
- Helpful

Staff should:

- Acknowledge the guest quickly
- Listen carefully
- Confirm what the guest needs
- Avoid unnecessary repetition
- Explain the next step
- Give a realistic response time
- Keep the guest updated
- Protect private information
- Confirm completion
- Record important requests and complaints

Staff should not:

- Argue with the guest
- Blame another staff member
- Make unsupported promises
- Share private information publicly
- Ignore delays
- Close an issue without confirmation
- Use insulting, defensive, or careless language

# Face-to-Face Greeting Standard

## Initial Greeting

Staff should:

1. Make respectful eye contact when appropriate.
2. Greet the guest politely.
3. Introduce themselves when useful.
4. Offer assistance.
5. Confirm the guest’s request.
6. Explain the next action.

## Example

> Good afternoon. Welcome to ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel. My name is [Name]. How may I assist you?

## When the Guest Is Waiting

> Thank you for waiting. I am checking this for you now and will update you as soon as I have confirmed information.

## When the Staff Member Does Not Know

> I do not want to give you incorrect information. Let me confirm this with the responsible team and return to you shortly.

# Telephone Standard

## Answering the Call

Staff should:

1. Answer politely.
2. Identify the hotel or department.
3. Introduce themselves.
4. Ask how they may help.
5. Confirm important details.
6. Record the request.
7. Explain the next step.
8. End politely.

## Example Opening

> Good morning, ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel front office. This is [Name] speaking. How may I assist you?

## Placing a Guest on Hold

Ask permission first:

> May I place you on hold briefly while I confirm that information?

Do not leave the guest on hold for an unreasonable time.

## Transferring a Call

Before transferring:

- Explain why
- Confirm the receiving department
- Avoid transferring the guest repeatedly
- Share only necessary information

## Ending the Call

> Thank you for contacting ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel. Please let us know if you need any further assistance.

# Email Standard

## Subject Line

The subject should be clear.

Examples:

- ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel Booking Confirmation
- Pre-Arrival Information Request
- Guest Request Follow-Up
- Complaint Resolution Update
- Transport Confirmation

## Email Structure

Use:

1. Greeting
2. Main purpose
3. Confirmed information
4. Required action or missing information
5. Deadline when needed
6. Contact information
7. Polite closing

## Example Booking Follow-Up

> Dear [Guest Name],
>
> Thank you for choosing ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel.
>
> To prepare for your arrival, please confirm your expected arrival time and whether you require airport transfer, accessibility support, dietary assistance, or any special room preparation.
>
> Kind regards,  
> ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel

# WhatsApp Standard

WhatsApp messages should be:

- Short
- Clear
- Professional
- Limited to necessary information
- Sent only through approved accounts
- Sent only when the guest has provided or accepted that channel

## Example

> Hello [Guest Name]. This is ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel. Please confirm your expected arrival time so we can prepare your room and arrival support. Thank you.

## WhatsApp Privacy Rule

Do not send:

- Full identification details
- Sensitive payment information
- Confidential complaint details
- Staff-confidential information
- Security instructions
- Access codes in an unsafe way

# Request Acknowledgement Template

> Thank you for your request. I have recorded it and assigned it to the responsible team. We expect to update you by [time].

# Delay Notification Template

> We are sorry for the delay. The request is still being handled by the responsible team. The updated expected completion time is [time]. We will continue to keep you informed.

# Missing Information Template

> Please confirm [missing information].
>
> This information is required because [reason].
>
> Once confirmed, we can continue with [affected action].

# Complaint Acknowledgement Template

> Thank you for telling us about this issue. We are sorry that your experience did not meet the expected standard. We have recorded the complaint and notified the responsible manager. We will update you by [time].

# Service-Recovery Update Template

> We have reviewed your concern and the approved action is [action]. The responsible team is working on this now, and we expect completion by [time].

Do not mention compensation unless it has been approved.

# Complaint Follow-Up Template

> We are following up regarding your recent concern. Please let us know whether the issue was resolved satisfactorily and whether you need any further assistance.

# Room-Not-Ready Template

> We are sorry that your room is not yet ready. Our team is completing the final preparation. The current expected readiness time is [time]. We can offer [approved waiting option] while you wait.

Do not promise a room completion time unless it has been confirmed.

# Maintenance Update Template

> The maintenance team has been informed about the issue in [room or area]. The current status is [status], and the expected next update is [time].

# Transport Confirmation Template

> Your transport has been confirmed.
>
> Pickup location: [location]  
> Pickup time: [time]  
> Driver or provider: [name]  
> Contact: [approved contact]

# Booking-Change Template

> We have recorded your booking-change request. The front-office team will check availability, the updated rate, and applicable conditions before confirming any change.

# Payment Clarification Template

> We are checking the payment information linked to your booking. We will confirm the correct status after reviewing the approved records.

Do not request sensitive card information through unapproved channels.

# Check-Out Message Template

> We hope you enjoyed your stay. Please let us know your expected departure time and whether you require luggage or transport assistance.

# Post-Stay Thank-You Template

> Thank you for staying with ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel. We appreciate your visit and welcome any feedback that may help us improve our service.

# Escalation Language

## Amber

> This matter requires management attention because it may affect the guest experience or hotel operations. The responsible manager has been notified.

## Red

> This is an urgent safety, security, privacy, legal, or serious guest-impact issue. The related automated action has been stopped, and the responsible manager has been notified immediately.

# Difficult Conversation Standard

When the guest is upset:

1. Stay calm.
2. Listen fully.
3. Acknowledge the concern.
4. Avoid arguing.
5. Avoid blaming.
6. Confirm the facts.
7. Explain the next step.
8. Involve the responsible manager.
9. Record the conversation.
10. Follow up.

## Example

> I understand why this situation is frustrating. I have recorded the issue and am contacting the responsible manager now. I will update you as soon as I have confirmed the next action.

# Language Standard

Communication should:

- Use simple words
- Use short sentences
- Avoid technical terms
- Avoid internal abbreviations
- Avoid vague promises
- Avoid aggressive or defensive wording
- Match the guest’s preferred language when possible
- Use approved translations

# Privacy Standard

Staff and Lulu must:

- Confirm identity before sensitive actions
- Use only necessary guest information
- Avoid speaking room numbers and private details loudly
- Avoid sharing booking, payment, complaint, or identification details publicly
- Use approved communication systems
- Respect consent and channel preferences
- Record important communication when required

# Approval Rules

Human approval is required before communicating:

- Refund
- Discount
- Compensation
- Free service
- Room upgrade
- Rate exception
- Legal response
- Security disclosure
- Sensitive privacy response
- Policy exception

# Lulu AI Communication Rules

Lulu may:

- Prepare draft guest messages
- Personalise approved templates with verified information
- Detect missing information
- Identify required approvals
- Suggest response priority
- Prepare delay updates
- Prepare complaint summaries
- Track communication deadlines
- Record approved messages

Lulu must not:

- Send high-risk external messages without approval
- Invent guest details
- Promise compensation
- Confirm unavailable information
- Share restricted information
- use an unapproved communication channel
- hide uncertainty or delay

# Communication Quality Checklist

Before sending a guest message, confirm:

- [ ] Guest or recipient is correct
- [ ] Information is verified
- [ ] Privacy is protected
- [ ] Tone is respectful
- [ ] Main answer appears early
- [ ] Required action is clear
- [ ] Deadline or update time is included
- [ ] Approval was obtained when required
- [ ] No unsupported promise is included
- [ ] Message is recorded when necessary
