# In-Stay, Guest Requests, Complaints, and Check-Out Workflows

## Purpose

This note defines how ONIRIA Stone Town Hotel supports guests during their stay, handles requests and complaints, completes check-out, manages lost property, and performs post-stay follow-up.

The workflow connects front office, housekeeping, maintenance, food and beverage, spa, transport, security, management, rooms, payment, privacy, and Lulu AI.

# Workflow Overview

```text
Guest checked in
→ In-stay support
→ Guest request received
→ Request assigned and tracked
→ Complaint handled when required
→ Service recovery
→ Check-out preparation
→ Payment and departure
→ Room-status update
→ Post-stay follow-up

Part 2: Complaint and Service-Recovery Workflow
Objective

Handle guest dissatisfaction fairly, quickly, respectfully, and consistently.

Cleanliness

Step 1: Listen

Staff should:

Allow the guest to explain
Avoid interrupting
Stay calm
Avoid arguing
Avoid assigning blame
Record the facts accurately
Step 2: Acknowledge

Use respectful language.

Example:

I understand that this experience has been frustrating. Thank you for explaining what happened.

Step 3: Apologise Appropriately

Example:

We are sorry that the service did not meet the expected standard.

An apology should not include an unsupported legal admission.

Step 4: Record the Complaint

Record:

Severity
Evidence

Immediate action

Step 5: Assess Severity
Green

Minor inconvenience that can be resolved through a routine service action.

Amber

Significant delay, repeated failure, billing concern, room problem, or guest dissatisfaction.

Red

Safety, security, privacy, discrimination, harassment, injury, legal risk, or serious repeated failure.

Step 6: Investigate

Check:

Staff notes
Room records
Maintenance records
System logs

Relevant SOP
